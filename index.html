About the Project

InternetSafetyAdventures is an open educational project that helps children and families learn about internet safety through interactive web simulations. Each activity mimics a familiar online scenario, revealing how to recognize safe versus unsafe digital behaviors.

Designed for classrooms, parents, and independent learners, these activities are fully self-contained — no installation, servers, or data collection required. Everything runs directly in the browser.

Educational Intent

The project was created to make digital safety education engaging and experiential. Each simulation provides a safe space for children to make small, guided mistakes and then reflect on what happened. This promotes lasting understanding rather than rote memorization.

  • Encourage critical thinking online.
  • Teach awareness of deceptive design and phishing tactics.
  • Promote discussion between teachers, parents, and students.

Interactive Activities

WrongLink

A simulation of a fake streaming site. Children click what appears to be a normal “Watch” button, but are redirected to a teaching page explaining how misleading links can appear trustworthy and how to check URLs safely.
